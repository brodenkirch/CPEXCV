Data Availability Statement:

Information on the NASA CPEX-CV 2022 campaign is available via Zawislak et al. (2022) and Nowottnick et al. (2024), the former of which includes a link to the main CPEX-CV campaign webpage. Direct links to the CPEX-CV instrument data, including the APR-3, DAWN, and dropsonde data used for the analysis, are also found through Zawislak et al. (2022). The CPEX-CV instrument data is freely accessible upon registering at the NASA EarthData repository.
 
TPW data used in the analysis is publicly available via Cooperative Institute for Meteorological Satellite Studies (2025), as is the IMERG and TIMPS data used for analysis through the Goddard Earth Sciences Data and Information Services Center (Huffman et al., 2019) and Russell (2022), respectively. ECMWF ERA5 component wind data and AEW tracker data used for manual lower tropospheric dynamical convergence calculations and analysis is publicly available through the Copernicus Climate Change Service Climate Data Store (Hersbach et al., 2018) and Lawton (2024), respectively. The Sounding/Hodograph Analysis and Research Program in Python (SHARPpy) open-source meteorological package used to calculate PBL depth and wind shear from CPEX-CV in situ observations is publicly available via Blumberg et al. (2017).
